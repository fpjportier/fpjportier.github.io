### On the Fragility of the Nonlinear Phillips Curve View of Recent Inflation

with Paul Beaudry and Sev Hou — *February 2025*  
[Latest version (pdf)](https://fportier.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/02/nlpc.v2.pdf)

**Abstract:** The paper examines whether the US evidence in favour of a nonlinearity in the Phillips curve is robust or fragile. Using both cross-city and aggregate time series data, and controlling carefully for inflation expectations, we find that the evidence in support of a nonlinear Phillips curve is very fragile.

### Some Inference Perils of Imposing a Taylor Rule

with Paul Beaudry and Andrew Preston — *March 2023*  
[Latest version (pdf)](https://fportier.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/taylor-rule.v2.3-1.pdf) | [CEPR DP18001](https://cepr.org/publications/dp18001)

**Abstract:** We show that assuming monetary authorities follow a Taylor rule may bias estimation of New Keynesian models for two reasons: a standard misspecification bias, and a more subtle determinacy bias. Using US data, the determinacy bias is shown to be a serious problem in small-scale New Keynesian models, biasing upward the slope of the Phillips curve.

### Dynamic Identification in VARs

with Paul Beaudry, Fabrice Collard, Patrick Fève and Alain Guay — *November 2022*  
[Latest version (pdf)](https://fportier.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/zvar.v3.2.pdf) | [CEPR DP17726](https://cepr.org/publications/dp17726)

**Abstract:** We prove that the dynamic structure of DSGE models brings enough restrictions to generically identify structural shocks in a VAR (D-SVAR). We show that D-SVAR estimation is a hidden step in full-information DSGE estimation and illustrate the framework with examples.

### Dynamic Cooper and John Economies

with Dana Galizia and Paul Beaudry — *April 2022*

**Abstract:** We study equilibria emerging when one increases the level of interactions between agents in a dynamic extension of Cooper and John's (1988) model. Depending on payoff properties, interactions can produce hysteresis, limit cycles and indeterminacy.

### Monetary Policy and Herd Behavior in New-Tech Investment

with Olivier Loisel and Aude Pommeret — *2010*  
[Latest version (pdf)](https://fportier.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/lpp-v6.pdf)

**Abstract:** We study the role of monetary policy when asset-price bubbles may form due to herd behavior in investment in a new technology whose productivity is uncertain. A "leaning against the wind" monetary policy can prevent herd behavior and the resulting bubble, and may be preferable to laissez-faire in terms of ex ante welfare.
